Lionel Messi on Tuesday confirmed he “doesn’t think” he will play at another World Cup in an interview with Chinese media, after the 35-year-old led Argentina to victory at last year’s tournament in Qatar.<\/p>\n

“I have said several times before that I don’t think so, that that (2022) was my last World Cup,” he said when asked by China’s Titan Sports in a video interview if he might feature at the 2026 tournament in North America.<\/p>\n

“I will see how things go but in theory I don’t think I will be around for the next World Cup.”<\/p>\n

The seven-time Ballon d’Or winner is currently in Beijing where Argentina will play a friendly against Australia on Thursday.<\/p>\n

His comments come after he said earlier this year to Argentinian newspaper Ole that it would be “very difficult” to play at another World Cup.<\/p>\n

He will turn 39 during the next tournament.<\/p>\n

Messi last week announced he would join MLS side Inter Miami after his two-year deal with Paris Saint-Germain came to an end.<\/p>\n

Argentina will play Thursday’s friendly against Australia at the 68,000-capacity Workers’ Stadium in Beijing.<\/p>\n

The match is a repeat of the last-16 tie between the teams at the World Cup in Doha, which Argentina won 2-1.<\/p>\n
